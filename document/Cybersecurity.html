Cybersecurity included

New EU regulations and directives are increasing the requirements for the IT security of charging infrastructure: from August 2025, the Radio Equipment Directive (RED) in accordance with EN 18031 will be mandatory. It requires the protection of charging points with wireless connections such as RFID, WLAN or cellular communication against unauthorized access. This affects smart and app-controlled home chargers, for example. The Cyber Resilience Act (CRA) will supplement this with comprehensive security requirements for networked devices from December 2027. Charging points are therefore considered digital products. Their manufacturers must ensure security updates, vulnerability management and CE marking. But don’t worry: we keep your charging controller compliant and secure with regular and free security updates to minimize the risk of cybersecurity attacks and tampering.
